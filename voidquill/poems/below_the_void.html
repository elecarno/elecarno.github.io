Below the Void

by Astoria Voidquill

Choked within the fog
I feel the structure rattle,
and rattle does my mind
—for structure I must battle.

I hear the breaking stone below,
I hear red thunder crack.
As every moment passes
and I must not look back.

'Void' — the crest I bear in name,
the endless swallows all.
'till beneath the black below the well
in places sailors
can only tell,
there is no home, there is an end.
and the dark will never be your friend.

2220.11.08

About

The first published piece by Astoria Voidquill. It describes her first night in the Avalon system during a heavy storm.
