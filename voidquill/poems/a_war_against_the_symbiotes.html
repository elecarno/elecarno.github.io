A War Against The Symbiotes

by Dahlia Voidquill

A spacer — deck officer — launches himself down the
steel corridor and disappears up the ladder shaft.
The drives are louder than cannons, the snap of their ignition
piercing like a thunderclap directly against one's ears,
the great plume too searing the eyes of any unlucky
sailor that happened to gaze upon the nozzle.

An alarm sounds, one of twenty that each of the crew
have memorised, the minute difference in length or tone
dictating the actions one may take to save their life.
The hull shudders, and the meaning of the
red lights above becomes painfully apparent.

But another sound rings throughout the cold metal,
undulating, writhing, splaying itself across the colours of
patriotism and supposed valor.

But this war does not carry ideals,
it is not fought for glory nor conquest,
but because it must be fought.

A steel panel is torn away, a great purple tendril
casting it to the depths of the blue.
A sailor ripped from his pseudo-home,
down to Hale, where mercy shall never grace his
soul once more.

c. 2200

About

"This poem is believed to have been written during one of the key assaults on The One that took place at the planet of HaleE2 in the atk-07 system. It is unknown which ship she was present on, however her vague description of the significance of its colours suggests that is was likely a recognisable Federation craft. It is also unknown exactly why she was present on this ship, however our leading theory is that she was assigned to the mission with the express intent of documenting the experience of fighting the Symbiotes."

— Voidquill Archive entry 2222.08.07 written by Astoria Voidquill
